Travel Guide

Everything you need to know for a smooth and enjoyable trip to Benguet Province

By Bus

The most common way to reach Benguet from Manila

  • Victory Liner, Genesis, Joy Bus: Depart from Cubao, Pasay, or Manila terminals
  • Travel time: 6-7 hours (depending on traffic and route)
  • Fare: ₱450-800 (regular to deluxe)
By Private Vehicle

Three main routes:

  • Kennon Road: Scenic but winding; often closed during rainy season
  • Marcos Highway (via Baguio): Most stable route
  • Halsema Highway: For Mountain Province destinations
By Plane + Bus

Fly to Clark International Airport (Pampanga), then take a bus to Baguio (2-3 hours)

Budget Options (₱500-1,500 per night)
  • Hostels and dormitories
  • Budget hotels and inns
  • Homestays with local families
Mid-Range Options (₱1,500-4,000 per night)
  • Comfortable hotels with amenities
  • Bed and breakfast establishments
  • Transient houses
Luxury Options (₱4,000+ per night)
  • Premium hotels and resorts
  • Boutique hotels with mountain views
  • Private vacation homes and villas
Booking Tips:
  • Book in advance during peak season (December-February)
  • Check for proximity to main attractions
  • Many accommodations offer free breakfast

Daily Budget Breakdown
Accommodation ₱800-3,000
Meals (3x a day) ₱500-1,200
Transportation ₱200-500
Activities/Entrance Fees ₱300-800
Total per day ₱1,800-5,500
Money-Saving Tips
  • Eat at local carinderias for authentic and cheap meals
  • Use jeepneys and tricycles instead of taxis
  • Visit free attractions like parks and viewpoints
  • Shop at public markets for souvenirs
  • Travel during off-peak season for lower prices

Peak Season (November - February)

Best weather with cool, dry conditions

  • Temperature: 10-20°C
  • Pros: Perfect weather, sea of clouds at Mt. Pulag
  • Cons: Crowded, higher prices
Flower Season (February - March)

Panagbenga Festival and blooming flowers

  • Temperature: 12-22°C
  • Pros: Colorful festivals, flower gardens in full bloom
  • Cons: Very crowded during festival week
Summer (March - May)

Warmer but still pleasant

  • Temperature: 15-25°C
  • Pros: Ideal for hiking, less crowded
  • Cons: Hotter than usual for Benguet
Rainy Season (June - October)

Lush greenery but wet

  • Temperature: 15-23°C
  • Pros: Lowest prices, verdant landscapes
  • Cons: Frequent rain, some roads may be closed, landslide risks

Health Reminders
  • Bring warm clothing; temperatures can drop significantly
  • Stay hydrated, especially during treks and hikes
  • Bring medication for altitude sickness if climbing Mt. Pulag
  • Apply sunscreen; UV rays are stronger at higher altitudes
  • Drink only bottled or purified water
Travel Safety
  • Check road conditions, especially during rainy season
  • Keep valuables secure in crowded areas
  • Inform someone of your itinerary, especially for treks
  • Hire registered guides for mountain activities
  • Keep emergency numbers handy
Important Notice: Always check weather forecasts and local advisories before traveling. Some routes may be closed during typhoons or heavy rain.
